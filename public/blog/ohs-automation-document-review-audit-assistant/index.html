On‑Demand Compliance: How an OHS Consultant Scaled Document Review & Audit Support Without Fixed Overhead

Published June 15, 2026 · By Alfred Corp · 7 min read

A solo Occupational Health & Safety (OHS) consultant specializing in industrial COR (Certificate of Recognition) compliance faced a harsh seasonal bottleneck. During the peak safety audit season, the sheer volume of mandatory document reviews—checking hazard assessments, toolbox talks, and training certificates against strict regulatory matrices—forced the consultant to hire expensive, short-term junior staff. This eroded margins and introduced significant quality control risks. By partnering with Alfred Corp, the consultant replaced the entire junior staffing cycle with an automated, AI-driven ingestion and review pipeline, successfully scaling output by 500% while maintaining absolute solo control.

The Problem

  • Seasonal Bottleneck: COR audits cluster at specific times of the year, creating overwhelming document spikes that could not be handled by one person.
  • High Cost of Temporary Labor: Hiring junior safety officers for 6-week contracts was expensive, required extensive onboarding, and often resulted in missed details due to human fatigue during repetitive document scanning.
  • Margin Compression: The overhead associated with managing contractors and subscribing to multiple point-solution SaaS tools (document storage, OCR, audit tracking) consumed up to 40% of the peak season revenue.

The Alfred Corp Approach

Phase 1: Regulatory Ingestion

Alfred Corp constructed a secure, client-facing portal allowing the consultant's clients to drop raw, unstructured files (PDFs, images, Excel) directly into the system. The platform utilized intelligent OCR to automatically extract text, dates, signatures, and safety topics.

Phase 2: Autonomous Scaling

We developed a custom document evaluation matrix within the AI agent. This agent automatically cross-referenced the extracted data against the specific provincial COR regulatory requirements, immediately flagging missing signatures, expired certifications, and incomplete hazard assessments before human review.

Phase 3: Human-in-the-Loop

The consultant's role shifted from manual document scanning to final-stage verification. Alfred Corp presented the consultant with a unified dashboard containing pre-audited files, highlighting only the exceptions and non-compliant items for professional sign-off.
