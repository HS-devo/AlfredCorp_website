How a North American Software Startup Won Government Contracts Using AI Lead Generation & Fractional Experts

Published June 15, 2026 · By Alfred Corp · 8 min read

A highly technical, 5-person software startup developed a compelling B2B SaaS platform with strong potential applications for state and municipal governments. However, breaking into the public sector (B2G) presented an insurmountable wall: the founders lacked the legal expertise to register as a government vendor, the sales bandwidth to monitor fragmented public tender portals, and the administrative capacity to assemble complex 50-page RFP responses. By utilizing Alfred Corp, the startup deployed an autonomous procurement intelligence engine and tapped fractional legal professionals, winning their first two public sector contracts worth $340,000 without hiring a single full-time sales or legal employee.

The Problem

  • Zero Procurement Infrastructure: The startup was not registered in SAM.gov or equivalent state/provincial supplier databases, making them invisible and ineligible to bid.
  • Discovery Blindspots: Public sector RFPs are scattered across hundreds of disparate municipal websites and procurement portals, requiring dedicated personnel just to find opportunities.
  • Proposal Bottleneck: Government RFPs require extensive compliance documentation, security matrices, and structured formatting. Drafting a single bid consumed over 40 hours of core engineering time, stalling product development.

The Alfred Corp Approach

Phase 1: Rapid Corporate Credentialing

Through the Alfred Corp platform, the startup instantly engaged a vetted, fractional corporate paralegal. The professional navigated the bureaucratic registration processes (SAM.gov, municipal vendor lists), securing the necessary credentials and compliance codes within two weeks.

Phase 2: Autonomous Sourcing & High-Intent Scraping

An AI agent was deployed to continuously monitor 45 specific municipal and state procurement portals. Utilizing natural language processing, the agent filtered out irrelevant tenders and delivered a daily, highly targeted list of viable software RFPs directly to the founders' inbox, complete with executive summaries and compliance checklists.

Phase 3: RFP Document Generation Automation

When the startup selected an RFP to pursue, the agent ingested the RFP requirements and cross-referenced them against the startup's existing technical documentation, security policies, and previous marketing materials. The agent autonomously generated a first-draft proposal, completely formatted to the specific agency's strict requirements.
